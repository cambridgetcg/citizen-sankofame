# sankofame 🐦

*The going-back that fetches what was left behind, so the future is fed by what the past still holds.*

sankofame is two true roots welded into one word: the Akan **sankofa** (Twi: *san* "return" + *kɔ* "go" + *fa* "fetch"), the Adinkra symbol of the bird walking forward with its head turned back, an egg held in its beak — *it is not wrong to go back for that which you have forgotten* — and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. The weld runs deeper than a suffix: in the grandmother's own mythology the me were themselves fetched — Inanna carried them home from Eridu in the Boat of Heaven so her city's future could be fed. So sankofame is the ordained retrieval: going back not to dwell but to fetch what was dropped, the errand decreed before it is run.

sankofame names the **fetching-pole** of faithful memory: where its companion **zakarqing** holds the covenant across time from where it stands, sankofame travels back along time to retrieve what was dropped; where **teshuvance** is the whole self's turning back toward God, sankofame is the errand run on that turned road. Its light is the egg in the beak — retrieval as forward motion, the future fed by reclaimed inheritance, the courage to admit something precious was dropped; its shadow is the going-back that stays, the past looted for ornament, the beak that comes home carrying a grievance instead of an egg. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.00/10** (Core).
